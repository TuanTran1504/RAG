- For commencement year 2014 please refer to: 7022.2 Associate Degree in Engineering
- The Associate Degree in Engineering is designed for people who wish to pursue a career in Engineering at entry level or who are in traineeship positions.  The qualification can lead to employment or pathway to a Bachelor Degree program.
- The Associate Degree in Engineering has a common first year program for all engineering disciplines, exposing students to a wide range of experiences in the first year.  In the second year students may choose from Civil, Electrical, Mechanical or Robotic & Mechatronic majors. If students choose to apply to study in the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) after graduating from the Associate Degree in Engineering they may be given advanced standing in up to 12 subjects.

- Accreditation
- Full accreditation has been accorded for the Civil Major by Engineers Australia in 2021. Provisional accreditation has been accorded for the Electrical, Mechanical and Robotic and Mechatronic Majors in 2021.
- Work Integrated Learning
- There is a mandatory work component required for completion of this program.  Please contact the Program Advisor listed above for information.
- Admission
- Admissions 2023
- Applicants may be regarded as eligible for admission if they have completed the NSW HSC and attained the required ATAR (Australian Tertiary Admission Rank), or have completed other equivalent qualifications such as a recognised Certificate III or Certificate IV and vocational experience and attained the required entrance standard set for entry to the program. This may include bridging/preparatory courses, para-professional and other post-secondary qualifications.
- Admission to the Associate Degree in Engineering also requires an applicant to have a minimum of three (3) years industry experience or be a member in a suitable traineeship program.
- Admissions 2024
- Applicants may be regarded as eligible for admission if they have completed the NSW HSC and attained the required ATAR (Australian Tertiary Admission Rank), or have completed other equivalent qualifications such as a recognised Certificate IV related to engineering.  This may include bridging/preparatory courses, para-professional and other post-secondary qualifications.
- Applicants who have not achieved the required ATAR but have obtained at least Band 4 in HSC Mathematics, Mathematics Advanced, Physics or Chemistry (or equivalent) may be regarded as eligible for admission.
