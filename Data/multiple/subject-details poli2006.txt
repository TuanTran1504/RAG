Academic Handbook Current Edition POLI 2006 Politics of Australia and Asia Relations Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 100277 Coordinator David Walton Opens in new window Description In 2024, this subject is replaced by POLI 2008 - Transnational Diplomacy. In this subject students will become familiar with Australian diplomacy and appreciate the range of pressing historical, political and cultural issues that affect Australia's place in the Asia Pacific region. It explores the factors that have shaped Australia's relationships with key countries in the region and considers the vital impact of political actors and their ideological stances. In developing a depth of knowledge on the importance of Australia's regional policies, this subject provides students with the opportunity to be involved in a simulation task dealing with a 'hot topic' for Australia's relations within the Asia Pacific region. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: describe the continuity and change in Australian foreign policy towards the Asia Pacific region
identify both domestic and international factors that have shaped Australia's relations with Asian countries
critique the ideological approaches to Asian relations of the major Australian political parties
provide critical commentary on Australia's key relationships with Asia
conduct research on a hot topic for Australia's relations with Asia
articulate a clear and logical position on issues that affect Australia's relations with countries in the Asia Pacific region. Subject Content Australia and Asia in the post-war environment
The making of foreign policy
Australia-Asia relations during the Cold War

The new Asia Pacific politics of engagement
