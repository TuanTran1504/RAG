Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7064 Managing Project Teams and Stakeholders Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 301187 Coordinator Sepani Senaratne Opens in new window Description This subject is designed to provide students with essential behavioural knowledge areas for project management. Since project management occurs in a team setting, project managers need to master team leadership by understanding team dynamics and behaviour in different team settings, which is a main focus area in this subject. Students will also learn effective ways to identify and engage project stakeholders and communication concepts and tools that effectively influence stakeholders. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Analyse project team structures and team leadership roles
Appraise how teams are formed and developed in project contexts
Identify, categorise and prioritise project stakeholders
Develop a stakeholder management plan
Critically evaluate communication management processes in different project settings Subject Content Project organisation and teams
Team leadership
Project team dynamics
Identifying and dealing with project stakeholders
Project communication
