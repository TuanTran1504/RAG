Academic Handbook Current Edition FINC 7001 Communication and Ethics for Financial Planners Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 201008 Coordinator Nicole Ibbett Opens in new window Description Students are immersed in an ethical framework in which to practice an array of essential skills required, by financial advisers, to create and maintain professional client adviser relationships. The subject investigates the importance of interpersonal skills and various approaches to dealing with the broad needs of clients - communication, psychological aspects of client decision making, culture and the creation of trust in the adviser -client relationship. This subject also includes professional writing skills, team work, time management, financial literacy and the promotion of professional services. Assess the financial literacy of a client;
Evaluate and critically analyse the ethical considerations and implications of trust and culture as they relate to financial planning advice;
Apply and apprise appropriate skills and knowledge to work productively in a relevant discipline-related business workplace;
