Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7088 Strategic Management Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200794 Coordinator Mahesh Enjeti Opens in new window Description Strategic Management integrates business functional knowledge. The practical approach provides opportunities to use a range of strategic analysis tools and to engage in problem-solving (individually and collaboratively). Simulated strategic decision-making that includes data interpretation and generating options requires exercise of communication, research, and information literacy capabilities. This subject will allow MBA graduates to interpret data, generate strategic options and contribute to strategic decision-making in a number of organisational contexts. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Outline the development of strategy theory and practice;
Practice the skill of situational analysis using the insights gained from applying different theoretical lenses;
Apply knowledge from a range of functional disciplines in the interpretation of scenarios and the identification of options thus relating learning from other units in the MBA degree;
Develop an appreciation of the dynamic and contested nature of strategic management processes in organisational and industry contexts;
Critique the limitations of frequently cited theoretical explanations for organisation success and failure;
Analyse contemporary developments and practice in the field of strategy in the form of self-directed research (such as sustainability, governance, risk, ethics and innovation);
Critically reflect on strategic management as a process given the iterative and complex relationships between strategy, culture, ethics, structure, resources and environment. Subject Content - introduction to strategy
