Level Undergraduate Level 2 subject Restrictions Students must have successfully completed 40 credit points and must be enrolled in 1665 Bachelor of Community Welfare, 1666 Bachelor of Social Work or 1713 Bachelor of Criminal and Community Justice/ Bachelor of Social Work. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Critically analyse the structure of society with respect to power and disadvantage including the influence of gender, age, ability, sexuality, ethnicity and economic disadvantage.
Demonstrate an understanding of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander ways of knowing, being and doing in social work practice.
Develop foundational skills for social work and welfare practice.
Describe the social, political, legal, cultural and organisational contexts and systems and how they impact on people.
Identify a practice framework that draws on contemporary theory, knowledge, methods and professional values.
Critically evaluate the challenges and opportunities for social work practice within a global and environmental context. Subject Content 1. Child care and child safety
