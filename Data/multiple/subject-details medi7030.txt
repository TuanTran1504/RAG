Academic Handbook Current Edition MEDI 7030 Research Project in Rural Generalist Medicine Credit Points 20 Legacy Code 401275 Coordinator Andrew Page Opens in new window Description This subject is an essential part of the final year of the Masters of Medicine (Rural Generalism). It will build on the students' knowledge and skills gained in prior subjects and the aim of this subject is to design and conduct a research project from an area relevant to their practice of rural generalism. Successful completion of the subject results in the composition of a report, and in an oral presentation, which will include details of the aims, methods, results and conclusions of the project. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Evaluate the theories and real-life issues relevant to rural medical practice
Design and conduct a research project in a rural and remote context
Communicate the results of the project to peers and other health professionals in oral and written forms in a professional manner
Reflect on own learning and professional development needs
Apply technology and self-directed learning strategies to enhance evidence-based practice. Subject Content Online Tutorial 1: Introduction to the Unit outlining Objectives and Timelines ULO 1,2,3,4,5
