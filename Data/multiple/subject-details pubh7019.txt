Academic Handbook Current Edition PUBH 7019 Occupational Health Management Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 300391 Coordinator Evan Atlantis Opens in new window Description This subject focuses on the practice of occupational health within national and international frameworks.  It explores issues such as the physical, social and mental impact of the occupational environment on health and includes the human body's response to occupational health hazard exposure through toxicological and epidemiological principles.  Strategies for the management of occupational health are examined, together with methods of monitoring and evaluating occupational health programs. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Critically discuss the potential impact of current work health and safety issues or emerging issues on worker health and wellbeing
Demonstrate an understanding of the health and science knowledge and practice underpinning occupational health management
Determine the needs of a given workplace to provide an environment that is not detrimental to the health of people entering that environment
Determine the strategies required to implement improvement in occupational health of an organisation, including health promotion, health surveillance and rehabilitation
Monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of occupational health programs in the workplace
Demonstrate appropriate postgraduate skills in researching and academic writing Subject Content Safety Management
