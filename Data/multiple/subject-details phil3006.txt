Academic Handbook Current Edition PHIL 3006 Books that Changed how we Think Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 102570 Coordinator Norma Lam-Saw Opens in new window Description This subject involves an in-depth study of a philosophical text that has reshaped our understanding about the world we live in. The close reading and discussion will develop the students' capacity to read and think deeply about particular problems. Students will follow the text step by step, gaining insights as to why it has had such a lasting influence. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Identify the major philosophical arguments in the text studied
Analyse the philosophical reasoning employed
Outline the importance of the philosopher and text to philosophical tradition
Evaluate the major achievements and any significant problems generated from the philosopher's contributions
Identify relevant cultural and social contexts of the philosopher and texts
Compose sound philosophical argument Subject Content Close reading of a major philosophical text by a single philosopher
Study of the relevance of that text to the relevant philosophical tradition in which it is located
Critical study of achievements and/or problems of the philosophical position
Situating the text in relevant cultural and social contexts Assessment The following table summarises the standard assessment tasks for this subject. Please note this is a guide only.
