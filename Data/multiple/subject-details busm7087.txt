Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7087 Strategic Employment Relations Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200722 Coordinator George Lafferty Opens in new window Description This capstone subject aims for students to acquire the skills, knowledge and understanding of the challenges of managing people strategically in complex and turbulent environments.  The subject examines theories on business strategy, strategic management, human resource strategy and industrial relations strategy. The use of knowledge of Human Resource Management and Industrial Relations in strategic analysis and evaluation for transforming the people management function is explained. Management practice is considered through examining ways of acting strategically relative to tendencies for the human resource function to become mired in tactical responses.  The tools and techniques for analysing, implementing and evaluating strategy are emphasised. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Explain approaches to theories of organisational strategy and structure through contrasting a range of influential models;
Design strategies in relation to stakeholders and context using case examples;
Critically analyse various employment relations theories and models that seek to explain the processes of strategy formation;
Relate management, union and government policies to the wider strategic concerns of the organisation with special consideration of how strategic decisions are made and implemented;
Analyse the development and implementation of employment relations strategy within the environment of business planning and in relation to a specific organisation for organisational transformation;
Apply tools for strategy implementation and evaluation. Subject Content - strategic management
