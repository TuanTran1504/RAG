- Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Sciences) (exit only) (4789)
- Approved Abbreviation:
- Western Sydney University Program Code:
- AQF Level:
- CRICOS Code: 108484M
- 4789 Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Science) is an early exit point for students who have completed three years of study from the following Programs: 4708 Bachelor of Podiatric Medicine, 4706 Bachelor of Physiotherapy, 4763 Bachelor of Speech Pathology, 4711 Bachelor of Occupational Therapy.
- Study Mode
- Three years full-time.
- Program Advice
- HealthSciences@westernsydney.edu.au
- Location
- Recommended Sequence
- Early Exit from 4708 Bachelor of Podiatric Medicine – 4789 Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Science)
- Qualification for this award requires the successful completion of 240 credit points as per the recommended sequence below.
- Early Exit from 4706 Bachelor of Physiotherapy – 4789 Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Science)
- Qualification for this award requires the successful completion of 240 credit points as per the recommended sequence below.
- Suggested Minors
- Health Promotion, Minor
- Physical Activity and Health, Minor
- Health and Recreation, Minor
- Early Exit from 4763 Bachelor of Speech Pathology – 4789 Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Science)
- Qualification for this award requires the successful completion of 240 credit points as per the recommended sequence below.
- Suggested Minors
- Culture and Society, Minor
- Child and Community, Minor
- Indigenous Australian Studies, Minor
- Linguistics, Minor
- Public Health, Minor
- Health Practice Management, Minor
- Alternate Subject Pool
- Early Exit from 4711 Bachelor of Occupational Therapy – 4789 Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Science)
- Qualification for this award requires the successful completion of 240 credit points as per the recommended sequence below.
- Early Exit from 4710 Bachelor of Traditional Chinese Medicine – 4789 Bachelor of Health Science (Clinical Science)
- Qualification for this award requires the successful completion of 240 credit points as per the recommended sequence below.
