Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7081 Reward Management Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200721 Coordinator Khalil Al Jerjawi Opens in new window Description This subject enables employment relations professionals to deploy advanced practitioner skills in specific workplace and institutional contexts. The management and the negotiation of reward are emphasised.  Innovations such as strategic and total reward strategies are critically examined.  These innovations relate to the key issues of market-imperatives and fairness, including the balance between collective and individually-determined reward and reward strategies related to performance. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Contrast, analyse and utilise the research literature to facilitate the analysis of reward management;
Apply multidisciplinary and competing stakeholder perspectives in debates and analysis of contemporary reward management;
Critically analyse reward and performance management debates and evaluate a range of policy and practice approaches;
Apply advanced reward practitioner skills in both workplace and institutional settings, in reward design and the negotiation of reward;
Analyse the negotiation of reward and the dynamics of disputes that arise within reward management. Subject Content - Reward policy and Governance
