Academic Handbook Current Edition ELEC 3003 Digital Signal Processing Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 300069 Coordinator Jeffrey Zou Opens in new window Description Students will develop an understanding of the fundamental concepts and principles in digital signal processing by applying the theory learned in their classes to practical exercises. The subject matter includes discrete-time signals and systems, the z-transform, sampling of continuous-time signals, transform analysis of linear time-invariant systems, filter design techniques, structures for discrete-time systems, the discrete Fourier transform and computation of the discrete Fourier transform. ELEC 2013 Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Analyse discrete-time signals and systems;
Apply the z-transform to signal processing;
Explain the sampling of continuous-time signals;
Apply filter design techniques to the design of discrete-time filters;
Analyse structures for discrete-time systems;
