Academic Handbook Current Edition PERF 2033 Recording Studio Practices Credit Points 10 Coordinator Noel Burgess Opens in new window Description This subject builds on concepts and practical work from Music Production and Sound Design. It prepares students creatively and technically to undertake self-directed technology-based projects as composers, sound designers and producers. Students will study professional-level sound studios, multi-track, hard disk recording systems, critical listening, and principles of sonic composition and studio production. Students will propose and complete a major studio-based creative project. School Humanities & Comm Arts Discipline Music Student Contribution Band HECS Band 2 10cp Check your fees via the Fees page. Level Undergraduate Level 2 subject Pre-requisite(s) PERF 1019 OR PERF 1018 OR PERF 1025 Equivalent Subjects PERF 2009 PERF 2013 PERF 2022 PERF 2026 PERF 2031 Assumed Knowledge Students enrolling in this subject are assumed to have a basic working knowledge of computer music and sound recording technologies and concepts, and safe working practices. Learning Outcomes After successful completion of this subject, students will be able to: Apply conventional studio production methods
Identify and employ recording studio production technologies
Demonstrate and continue to develop critical listening skills
Design, plan and realise creative production projects
