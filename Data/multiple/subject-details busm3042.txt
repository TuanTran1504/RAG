Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 3042 Managing Diversity Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200150 Coordinator Louise Ingersoll Opens in new window Description This subject explores the complexities and challenges of managing diverse workforces in contemporary organisations. Using applied learning approaches, students are required to formulate corporate policies relating to diversity and then evaluate the implications for implementing these policies in a real world setting. As a key component of human resource management, students are expected to appreciate both the theoretical and practical elements of managing diversity and are required to reflect on their own learning process throughout the subject. Level Undergraduate Level 3 subject Pre-requisite(s) BUSM 1023 Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Assess the nature of workforce diversity and the drivers of its management;
Formulate corporate policy relating to diversity management;
Analyse the implications for implementing diversity initiatives and the link to competitive advantage;
Examine key challenges in managing a diverse workforce;
Evaluate the sources of and potential for conflict in diverse workplaces;
