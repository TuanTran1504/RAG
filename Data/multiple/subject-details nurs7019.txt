Academic Handbook Current Edition NURS 7019 Cultural and Social Diversity in Health Care Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 401082 Coordinator Fung Koo Opens in new window Description This subject encourages students to investigate and discuss the concepts of health and illness in the context of cultural and social diversity. The subject promotes the attainment of critical knowledge and core principles necessary for students to become holistic, culturally and socially sensitive and appropriately adaptable when meeting the health needs of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, other marginalised and vulnerable groups including refugees and those from LGBTI community, and people from culturally and linguistically diverse (CALD) backgrounds. A multicultural approach provides a framework for students to explore the impact of different attitude and value systems relating to the health of peoples in Australia. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Critique the social determinants of health among culturally and socially diverse communities
Critically reflect on own values and attitudes when working in culturally and socially diverse communities
Apply integrated knowledge of culture, ethnicity and diversity into the provision of health care for a multicultural society
Differentiate health related risk factors for people from diverse cultural groups
