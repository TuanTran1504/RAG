Academic Handbook Current Edition WELF 2009 Human Rights, Human Services and the Law (WSTC) Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 700141 Coordinator Paul Rouillon Opens in new window Description This subject aims to provide knowledge of human rights issues and laws which are particularly relevant to social workers, youth workers and community welfare workers. It provides an opportunity for students to reflect upon their powers, responsibilities and obligations in professional roles and acquire skills to promote justice for disadvantaged individuals and groups through advocacy and law reform. Level Undergraduate Level 2 subject Equivalent Subjects WELF 2008 - Human Rights Human Services and the Law Restrictions Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Identify and describe features of the Australian legal system with particular reference to NSW;
Explain the legal institutions and processes which promote or undermine access to justice and law reform for disadvantaged groups in the community;
Outline areas of substantive law which are particularly relevant to social work, community welfare and youth work practice;
Apply advocacy skills using legal knowledge;
Analyse the impact of international human rights instruments and process;
Write a professional report for legal purposes. Subject Content - An overview of the Australian legal system with particular reference to NSW
