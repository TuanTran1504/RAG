Academic Handbook Current Edition LAWS 3082 Property Law Credit Points 10 Coordinator Pamela Devery Opens in new window Description This subject deals with concepts of property and the nature of property rights including the boundaries between property and other rights and the boundaries between different forms of property. The subject’s content includes the doctrine of tenure and estates, native title, and legal and equitable interests in land. Specific topics to be covered include personal property and securities, mortgages, leases, easements and profits a prendre, co-ownership, systems of statutory registration of interests in property and the principles for resolving conflicting proprietary interests (priorities). LAWS 2003 Contracts Equivalent Subjects LAWS 2011 (200012) Property Law Learning Outcomes After successful completion of this subject, students will be able to: Identify legal issues relating to property and explain and analyse concepts and principles of Australian property law.
Apply legislation and legal principles of Australian property law to provide advice in relation to proprietary interests and disputes.
Explain the development of property law, including Australian Native Title law, and critically evaluate its significance in a social justice framework.
Demonstrate an understanding of the operation of, and assumptions that underlie Australian property law in historical, political, social, economic, cultural and ethical contexts.
Display clear and logical structure, using accurate citation, grammar and punctuation in written work Subject Content The concept of property
The boundaries between different types of property
Personal Property and Securities
Possession and limitations of actions
Leases
Mortgages
Native Title
The acquisition and transfer of interests in land
Torrens title indefeasibility and exceptions
Priority ordering for the resolution of disputes between competing proprietary interests
