Academic Handbook Current Edition MEDI 7042 Diabetes Self-Management Support II: Systems and Organisations Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 401287 Coordinator Therese Fletcher Opens in new window Description The increasing number of people with diabetes in Australia and globally is a major public health issue. This subject will explore the increasing burden of diabetes, why some groups of people are more at risk of developing diabetes then others, how society and the way health services are organised and delivered influence the risk of developing diabetes, access to quality health care and individual health outcomes. This subject with examine social determinants related to diabetes, the impact of diabetes on Indigenous Australians, diabetes public policy, workforce capability and the integrated nature of service delivery in reducing prevalence and improving health outcomes for people with diabetes and prediabetes. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Examine the prevalence and economic burden of diabetes
Explore the broader social determinants and the organisation of health service delivery that influence diabetes prevalence, diabetes self-management and health outcomes for individuals and community
Appraise the high incidence of diabetes and diabetes related complications amongst Indigenous Australians and the provision of culturally appropriate health care
Analyse the impact of public health, non -health policies, and the role of primary health care in reducing diabetes prevalence and its complications
Analyse primary, secondary and tertiary prevention in diabetes and the importance of the integrated approach at the macro, meso and micro levels.
Critically evaluate the central role of primary care, general practice and interventions that maximise service delivery effectiveness
Critically appraise evidence and practice through research and quality improvement activities Subject Content Assessment The following table summarises the standard assessment tasks for this subject.
