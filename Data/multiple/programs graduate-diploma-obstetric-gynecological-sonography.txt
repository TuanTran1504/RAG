- Graduate Diploma in Obstetric and Gynecological Sonography
- This program applies to students who commenced in 2024 or later.
- The Graduate Diploma in Obstetric and Gynecological Sonography provides graduate entry training in obstetric and gynecological ultrasound imaging. It integrates online and on-campus learning activities with the students’ workplace training in sonography practice. The workplace training will consist at a minimum, of 24 hours a week for the duration of the program (equal to 2300 hours), which is the benchmark set to be considered eligible for accreditation with the Australian Sonographer Accreditation Registry. Students will develop theoretical, practical and professional skills that are essential for the practice of obstetric and gynecological sonography and for further professional development.

- Accreditation
- The Graduate Diploma in Obstetric and Gynecological Sonography program has Professional Accreditation with the Australian Sonographers Accreditation Registry (ASAR). Graduates are eligible to register as an accredited O&G sonographer.
- Applicants must have successfully completed an undergraduate degree in natural and physical sciences, or health.
- Applicants with an undergraduate degree in another field will be considered for admission if they can demonstrate greater than 12 months full time work experience in directly related obstetrics and gynecological assessment (which must be supported with a statement of service for all work experience listed).
- Applicants must secure a minimum of three days of work (training) in obstetric and gynecological sonography each week for the duration of the program. Written verification of work (training) must be provided to the university two weeks prior to the commencement of the program.
- Applicants seeking admission on the basis of work experience MUST support their application with a Statement of Service for all work experience listed on the application.
