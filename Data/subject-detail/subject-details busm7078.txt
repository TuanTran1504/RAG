Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7078 Project Management Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 51240 Coordinator Dilupa Nakandala Opens in new window Description This subject introduces students to the philosophy, tools and techniques for effectively managing projects in an organisation. Participants will be required to apply lessons learned to real-world situations and cases, developing their professional expertise in project management in ways that are relevant to their careers. Topics include organisational strategy and project selection; project definition and criteria of merit; tools and techniques; team leadership and management; development of project plans; estimation of time and resources; risk analysis and management; scheduling and control; resource allocation; project tracking; project closure and review. Participants will be expected to use MS Project as a project management tool. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Demonstrate an understanding of the role of project management and how projects link to corporate strategic planning and strategic management;
Apply knowledge and techniques on project planning;
Apply knowledge on project risk assessment and management;
Demonstrate knowledge on project progress controlling and monitoring;
Demonstrate an understanding of the importance of project completion and closure procedures;
Explain and apply the principles of managing project teams and resolving team conflicts. Subject Content Project life cycle, selection and initiation
