Academic Handbook Current Edition SPRT 1009 Exercise Performance and Health Credit Points 10 Coordinator Sarah Kennedy Opens in new window Description Students will learn the functional anatomy, basic exercise physiology, physical fitness, motor development and exercise training content required to practice as competent exercise/sport/health professionals. Students will also administer and undertake laboratory exercises designed to test, design and administer fitness programs for healthy individuals. Level Undergraduate Level 1 subject Incompatible Subjects SPRT 3001 Restrictions Students must be enrolled in 4741 - Bachelor of Sport Development, 4791 - Bachelor of Health and Physical Education (Pathway to Teaching Education), 4792 - Bachelor of Health and Physical Education, 6001 Diploma in Health Science/Bachelor of Health Science (Health and Physical Education), 4656 B Health Science, 1939 B Education (Secondary) Assumed Knowledge An understanding of basic human biology and fundamental principles in exercise science. Learning Outcomes Describe cardiorespiratory, neuromuscular, anaerobic and aerobic energy systems and explain their role in fitness and performance
Explain muscle physiology and contraction, including neural control 
Conduct exercise consultations, including suitable administration of exercise testing and to assess various components of physical fitness and performance
Develop exercise programs for healthy individuals to achieve desired performance and/or fitness gains
Implement an evidence-based approach to exercise/sport/health fitness and performance enhancement
Identify the key physiological differences between trained and untrained individuals
Discuss the use of ergogenic aids in sport and exercise Subject Content Neural control of muscle contraction
Muscle physiology and contraction
Aerobic and anaerobic energy systems
Cardiorespiratory physiology
Pre-exercise screening and consultation
Exercise testing for healthy clients
Exercise prescription (cardiorespiratory, resistance training, flexibility)
Personal experience of exercise testing and training
Physiological differences between trained and untrained individuals
