Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7062 Managerial Finance Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200787 Coordinator Walid Bakry Opens in new window Description This subject is designed to provide students with the knowledge and skills required for understanding, analysing and solving corporate financial management issues. The focus is corporate-oriented with emphasis on the practical application of economic theory in financial decisions confronting business today. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Demonstrate an understanding of the relevance of corporate finance to business practice;
Attain knowledge of the key issues facing financial managers;
Comprehend and apply finance theory underlying financial management;
Implement skills in corporate financial-decision making;
Be able to appraise the current financial environment in which business decisions are made. Subject Content Annual report, cash flow, role of time value in finance
The time value of money
Risk, return and the Security Market Line (SML)
Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)
Interest rates, bond valuation, valuing shares, and dividends
Long-term investing and capital budgeting
Net present value, other investment criteria, consideration of risk
