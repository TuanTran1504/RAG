Academic Handbook Current Edition HUMN 3093 War Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 102423 Coordinator Peter Mauch Opens in new window Description Have you ever wondered what war is good for? Edwin Starr thought it was good for "absolutely nothing." This subject will take you on an intellectual journey, around the globe and across history, as you reach your own answer to this question. You will evaluate the norms associated with war as well as the experiences of warriors from ancient through modern times. You will assess the role of militias, armies, navies, and air forces in the broad sweep of history. You will consider nuclear weaponry, terrorism, guerrilla warfare, just war theory, as well as anti-war movements. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Apply theoretical perspectives on warfare to historical case studies.
Critically assess the development of strategies and technologies of warfare.
Evaluate various popular and scholarly explanations of wars' causes.
Demonstrate an understanding of the connections between war and politics.
Identify the causes and nature of the key wars in history covered in the course.
Compare and contrast the varying perspectives on war in Western and Non-Western societies. Subject Content - The history of warfare: from Ancient times to The present
- The development of practices in warfare: Technology, armies, strategy, War economies
- The development of thought as It relates to War
- Global perspectives on War
- Protesting against War/depicting War
