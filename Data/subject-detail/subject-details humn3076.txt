Academic Handbook Current Edition HUMN 3076 Sultans, Colonists and Nationalists: Indonesia C1200-1942 Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 102187 Coordinator Karen Entwistle Opens in new window Description This subject examines the historical background of Indonesia's struggle to attain independent nationhood. Emphasis will be placed on social, cultural and political factors that shaped Indonesia prior to 1942, encompassing the classical period of the Hindu-Buddhist kingdoms, the rise of the early modern Islamic sultanates, the first encounters between Europeans and the peoples of the 'Malay world', the emergence of the Dutch East India Company (VOC) as a dominant force in the region, the subsequent imposition of the Dutch colonial rule, and, most significantly, the development of modern Indonesian nationalism. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Identify the significant historical forces that shaped Indonesia prior to 1942
Describe the cultural, religious and political diversity of Indonesian society
Recognise the factors that contributed to the rise and fall of traditional Indonesian polities during the pre-colonial era
Outline key differences between the Hindu-Buddhist and Islamic kingdoms
Discuss the pattern of Dutch colonial rule and changing economic structures in Indonesia
Evaluate the impact of modern and traditional influences on the development of Indonesian nationalism
Demonstrate effective literacy skills and research techniques. Subject Content - Indonesia as An archipelagic cross-roads
