Academic Handbook Current Edition LAWS 3029 Immigration Law Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200298 Coordinator Jason Donnelly Opens in new window Description This subject offers a comprehensive overview of immigration law, focusing on the Australian immigration system and visa processes. Students will explore general principles, visa applications, including family inclusion and health requirements, and public interest criteria. The subject also covers the legal aspects of losing a visa, immigration offences, and visa conditions. Additionally, students will learn about the review and cancellation of visas. This subject provides essential knowledge and skills for navigating the complexities of immigration law and policy, preparing them for relevant legal careers. Level Undergraduate Level 3 subject Pre-requisite(s) LAWS 2008 Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Analyse the structure and functioning of the Australian immigration system.
Explain the processes, requirements, and procedures involved in applying for visas within the Australian immigration system, including conditions for family members.
Examine the circumstances under which an Australian visa can be lost and the legal implications of immigration offences. 
Evaluate the use of public interest criteria in visa applications and health requirements in Australian immigration policy. 
Assess the procedures and legal frameworks for the review and cancellation of visas. Subject Content Assessment The following table summarises the standard assessment tasks for this subject.
