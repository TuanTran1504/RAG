Academic Handbook Current Edition INFS 1002 Information Systems in Context Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 300573 Coordinator Ana Hol Opens in new window Description This subject aims to give students the ability to recognise and explain business information systems with regard to type, function, purpose, and the frameworks within which these systems are used.  Topics in this subject include computing fundamentals; computer hardware and software; computers and society; use of business application packages - spreadsheets,  word processing, database, graphics; organisational information systems;   information systems development and acquisition; data and knowledge management; electronic commerce, internets, extranets;  networking; enterprise-wide information systems; the internet and information systems security; privacy, ethics and computer crime. Learning Outcomes Identify and explain an information system with regards to its type, functionality, role and impact;
Recognise and explain the role of management, information system professionals and end users when developing and implementing information systems in a business environment;
Discuss the implications of contemporary issues effecting business information systems;
Identify and explain the structure and business uses of data, information and knowledge banks;
Apply the knowledge of common business application packages to present information at a professional level;
Summarise, analyse and present data collected through research.
