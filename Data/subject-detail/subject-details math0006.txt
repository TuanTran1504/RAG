Academic Handbook Current Edition MATH 0006 Mathematics 1 (WSTC Prep) Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 700284 Coordinator Zdenka Misanovic Opens in new window Description This subject has been designed to enhance students' numeracy skills and their understanding of basic mathematical concepts taught in high school mathematics.  The topics include arithmetic and algebra, elementary functions, and basic geometry and trigonometry. The subject will prepare students and help them follow more advanced topics in Mathematics 2, Mathematics for Engineers Preliminary and Mathematics for Engineers 1, as well as various other Engineering and ICT subjects. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Perform operations and solve simple problems involving whole numbers, decimals, fractions and surds.
Apply algebraic rules and techniques to solve simple equations and manipulate formulas
Demonstrate conceptual understanding of a function, the domain and range, function graph, and the inverse function, and apply it to model and analyse relationships between quantities.
Relate functions (linear and quadratic) and the corresponding equations and apply the relationship to analyse engineering applications.
Apply the basic trigonometric ratios and Pythagorasï¿½fs theorem to solve problems involving. triangles.
Calculate areas and volumes. Subject Content 1. Arithmetic (numbers, operations with numbers including whole numbers, fractions, decimal and percentages, properties of real numbers). Measurements and units, estimates, scientific notation and rounding, uncertainties.
2. Algebra (algebraic rules and techniques, equations and identities, working with formulas).
3. Functions (definition and notation, domain and range, graphs, the inverse function).
4. Linear and Quadratics functions and equations; linear systems of 2 equations.
5. Basic Geometry and Trigonometry (angles, triangles, Pythagoras?fs theorem, trigonometric ratios).
6. Areas and Volumes.
