Academic Handbook Current Edition CMPL 2006 Traditional Chinese Medicine 3 Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 400352 Coordinator Xiaoshu Zhu Opens in new window Description This subject enables students to develop a sound understanding of causes of disease in TCM with a particular focus on TCM disease pattern differentiation. Students will be able to identify the clinical manifestations relating to specific patterns and develop the appropriate TCM treatment principles. This is complemented by the reinforcement of skills in case history taking and TCM diagnostics. School Health Sciences Discipline Traditional Chinese Medicine Student Contribution Band HECS Band 2 10cp Check your fees via the Fees page. Level Undergraduate Level 2 subject Pre-requisite(s) CMPL 1002 Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Differentiate TCM disease patterns according to the Eight Principles, zang-fu organs; Qi, Blood and body fluid; and the aetiology
Explain the progression of disease
Compare and contrast the mechanisms of aetiology and pathogenesis in TCM.
Articulate the treatment principles in TCM Subject Content 1. TCM pathophysiology: 
- organ (zang fu) pathophysiology 
- qi, blood, body fluid pathophysiology 
- channel pathophysiology 
- latent diseases 
2. Pattern differentiation in TCM: 
- according to pathogen (heat, damp, dry, cold, wind, phlegm, food and blood stassis) 
- according to the Eight Principle patterns of disease
- according to the zang fu organs 
- according to qi, blood and body fluid 
- Symptoms as a result of conflict between pathogenic and antipathogenic qi 
- Root and symptoms (ben and biao) - Disease progression and the transformation of disease states (six divisions, four stages, 3 levels (jiao))
