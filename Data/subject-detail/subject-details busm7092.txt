Academic Handbook Current Edition BUSM 7092 Supply Chain Management Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200329 Coordinator Muhammad Salman Asif Opens in new window Description With a rapid increase in global trade and increasing customer expectations, firms are under considerable competitive pressure to find cost-effective and creative ways of delivering value to customers. Since the creation of customer value needs to be viewed holistically - from raw material movement from suppliers through to transformation in the factory and then on to distributors and customers - the effective management of the supply chain and related business networks is critical to achieving competitive advantage. Through formal lectures, case study discussions, and assignments, this subject provides the foundational knowledge, tools, and techniques needed to participate in the design, implementation, and management of an effective supply chain. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Describe supply chain and logistics processes and explain the underlying principles that support their effective management;
Elaborate on the role and importance of customer relationship management and its importance in developing an effective and efficient supply chain;
Identify the various elements of supply chain and logistics management processes and critically evaluate their role and interdependence in effective supply chain management;
Interpret, from a strategic perspective, the role and importance of supply chain management in enhancing competitive advantage;
Define and evaluate the networks and relationships present within supply chains;
Apply important tools and techniques for designing, operating and evaluating the performance of an effective supply chain management system. Subject Content 21st century supply chains and logistics management
