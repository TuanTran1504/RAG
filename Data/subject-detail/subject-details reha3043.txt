Academic Handbook Current Edition REHA 3043 Recreational Therapy Internship Credit Points 20 Legacy Code 401400 Coordinator Arianne Reis Opens in new window Description This subject allows students to integrate and apply all learning accumulated throughout their Recreational Therapy major. It focuses on preparing students to professional practice by including 560h of internship in recreational therapy services in addition to coursework focusing on professional development. Students will have an opportunity to work on their professionalism, assessment skills, planning and implementation of recreation therapy programs and services, evaluation and documentation of client progress and work on their administration skills. This subject is designed to meet all National Council for Therapeutic Recreation Certification (NCTRC) requirements, leading to potential Certified Therapeutic Recreation Specialist (CTRS) designation. The internship may be in any area of recreation therapy practice that serves studentsâ€™ needs and accreditation requirements. REHA 2015 Restrictions To enrol in this unit, students must: Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Apply ethical conduct standards to recreation therapy practice
Consider the policy structures within which recreation therapy practice is situated as well as the political challenges facing the profession
Employ professional behaviours and attitudes in practice settings, including communicating effectively with clients, team members, relevant community groups and industry partners.
Utilise a range of assessments to set long- and short-term goals
Implement and evaluate recreational therapy interventions in the practice setting.
Prepare documents in a professional format
Practice self-directed learning strategies and reflect on practice experience to continually improve practice
Implement career planning strategies, including the development of professional networks and knowledge and skills for entering the workforce Subject Content 1.Professional practice placement
2.Professional behaviours and self-management skills
3.Communication skills
4.Documentation skills for practice
