Academic Handbook Current Edition PUBH 6004 Applied Epidemiology Research Design Credit Points 10 Coordinator Sam Hogan Opens in new window Description This subject is designed to extend students' practical application of different epidemiological study designs to give them a practical understanding of how epidemiological principles and practices are used in real world settings. Students should have basic knowledge and skills in epidemiology to be successful in this subject. Students will have the opportunity to acquire and apply the practical knowledge and skills needed to undertake epidemiological research to investigate contemporary local and global health issues. School Medicine Discipline Epidemiology Student Contribution Band HECS Band 2 10cp Check your fees via the Fees page. Level Postgraduate Coursework Level 6 subject Assumed Knowledge To be successful in this subject, students should have prior basic knowledge of key content delivered in PUBH7015 Introduction to Clinical Epidemiology or PUBH7016 Introduction to Epidemiology. Learning Outcomes Compare and contrast conceptual approaches to the conduct, design and analysis of epidemiological studies and research 
Analyse important methodological and practical issues involved in designing and conducting case-control studies, cohort studies and cross-sectional studies 
Explore the design aspects of ecological, spatial and genetic epidemiological studies
Examine the impact of chance and bias on study results and how this can be reduced through study design
Apply key reporting requirements of epidemiological studies
Reflect on personal and professional needs to enhance evidence-based practice in epidemiology Subject Content Selecting an epidemiological study design to investigate contemporary local and global health challenges
Working with stakeholders to design epidemiological studies
Ethical considerations in the design of epidemiological studies
Sampling approaches for epidemiological studies
Descriptive epidemiological study designs 
Prospective and retrospective cohort study designs 
Case control study designs
Ecological study designs
Spatial epidemiological study designs to describe geographic variation in disease
Genetic epidemiology approaches
Reporting epidemiological studies Special Requirements Essential equipment Laptop / device with stable internet connection.
