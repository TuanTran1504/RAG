Academic Handbook Current Edition PHYS 0001 Foundation Physics 1 (WSTC Prep) Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 700144 Coordinator Ben Kelley Opens in new window Description This subject replaces 700026 - Physics (UWSCFS) from Term 1 2014. This subject provides a brief introduction to the essentials of Physics. This subject is focused on skills and knowledge that students from a variety of science, construction and engineering courses need in their first year of study. Students cover introductory topics in Mechanics, Energy and Power, Electricity and waves. Learning Outcomes Use graphical and computer methods to analyse data
Identify the characteristics of uniform motion and calculate variables of motion in one dimension
Identify the characteristics of uniformly accelerated motion and predict variables of motion based on past or current conditions in one dimension
Use Newtonian dynamics to quantitatively analyse objects in equilibrium and acceleration
Use the concepts of work and conservation of energy to explain the behaviour of different systems
Demonstrate an ability to describe and apply quantitative relationship between charge, current, resistance and electrical power in the combined circuits
Quantitatively analyse reflection and refraction of waves
Perform experiments to demonstrate and measure physics principles and concepts Subject Content Assessment The following table summarises the standard assessment tasks for this subject.
