Academic Handbook Current Edition HUMN 3058 Islamic Revivalism in the Globalised World Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 102297 Coordinator Jan Ali Opens in new window Description In the last decades there has been a surge in Islamic consciousness in Musllim communities across the globe. Islamic history has been punctuated by periods of revivalist activity and its hallmark always has been a desire for the return to Islamic origins - the basics of the faith as enshrined in the islamic scripture. This subject explores the phenomenon of contemporary Islamic revivalism. The subject contrasts contemporary Islamic revivalism with earlier expressions. It aims to demonstrate that contemporary Islamic revivalism has manifested itself in a multiplicity of forms as a defensive reaction to an epoch characterised as modernity. Analyse scholarly debates surrounding contemporary Islamic revivalism
Assess the causes and consequences of contemporary Islamic revivalism
Examine the theoretical models of Islamic revivalism
Analyse the ideology, diversity and activities of Islamic revivalist movements
Apply conceptual and theoretical approaches to the understanding of Islam and Muslims in a globalised word
