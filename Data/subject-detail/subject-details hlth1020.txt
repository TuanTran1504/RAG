Academic Handbook Current Edition HLTH 1020 Foundations of HPE Credit Points 10 Coordinator Sarah Kennedy Opens in new window Description This subject introduces foundational disciplinary knowledge and elementary principles of practice within sport, health, and physical education across a variety of contexts. It aims to commence the development of the key professional practices of the field, where students will learn the basic processes of design, implementation, and evaluation inherent in the teaching and learning cycle. This will require students to engage in a variety of practical activities such that they begin to understand the essential requirements for creating learning experiences in HPE and sport settings. Learning Outcomes After successful completion of this subject, students will be able to: Identify how social justice principles can inform learning in, through and about health, physical education, and sport
Create positive, safe, and enjoyable learning climates for young people in health, physical education, and sport contexts 
Demonstrate an understanding of the essential features of designing, implementing and evaluating learning in health, physical education, and sporting contexts Subject Content Health and physical education: Where has it come from and where is it going?
 Models, styles, and principles of teaching and learning
 Justice, fairness, and equity as a framework for teaching and learning
 Creating positive and safe learning climates
 Planning for, leading, and assessing learning in movement activities
 Learning to be a scholar in the field
Child Protection training and Working with Children Check completion Special Requirements Essential equipment Attend practical sessions with appropriate clothing, footwear and sun protection.
