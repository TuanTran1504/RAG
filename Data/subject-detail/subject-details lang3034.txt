Academic Handbook Current Edition LANG 3034 Historical Linguistics Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 102043 Coordinator Robert Mailhammer Opens in new window Description This subject provides an introduction into the study of language change. It discusses fundamental questions such as how and why languages change, how we can investigate and theoretically capture language change, as well as how language change is connected to sociocultural change with special focus on the linguistic habitat of Australia. Assumed Knowledge Knowledge of fundamental linguistic terminology, introductory level linguistics; preferably phonetics/phonology and structure of language. Learning Outcomes demonstrate knowledge of the key concepts in historical linguistics, and explain this knowledge to others;
make connections between the topics in this subject and their significance within the discipline of historical linguistics, especially in relation to the linguistic ecology of Australia;
apply theoretical knowledge to the analysis of data;
explain the value and principles of research in historical linguistics. Subject Content Assessment The following table summarises the standard assessment tasks for this subject.
