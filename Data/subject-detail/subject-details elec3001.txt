Academic Handbook Current Edition ELEC 3001 Communication Systems Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 300007 Coordinator Khoa Le Opens in new window Description This subject will provide a basic introduction to communication systems and techniques.  Specific topics covered include energy and power spectral density, amplitude modulation, frequency modulation, pulse modulation, an overview of digital modulation techniques, noise in communication systems and an overview of current telecommunication systems; spread spectrum systems, optical communication systems, radio broadcasting and mobile communication systems. Level Undergraduate Level 3 subject Pre-requisite(s) ELEC 2011 Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Analyse analogue modulation techniques such as amplitude and frequency modulation
Analyse pulse modulation techniques, quantisation methods, delta modulation, and pulse code modulation
Evaluate analogue communication systems performance in the presence of noise
Describe basic digital modulation techniques
Apply modulation and Fourier transform techniques to explain spread spectrum systems and other mobile communication systems Subject Content Review of Fourier Series and Fourier Transforms
Continuous-wave modulation (AM, DSBSC, SSB, FM)
Pulse Modulation (PAM, PPM, PWM)
Delta Modulation
Digital Data Transmission
Introduction to Digital Modulation techniques
Introduction to spread spectrum communication systems
