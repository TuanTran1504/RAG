Academic Handbook Current Edition REHA 2014 Recreational Therapy Assessment and Documentation Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 401399 Coordinator Arianne Reis Opens in new window Description This subject focuses on the theoretical and practical aspects associated with the planning, implementation and management of assessments and evaluation in recreational therapy services, including all documentation needs of the profession. Students will learn all components of assessments as they are connected to a comprehensive program plan and will develop their recreational therapy assessment and evaluation skills.
Students will be required to complete an 80-hour industry-based placement as part of the requirements for this subject. School Health Sciences Discipline Rehabilitation Therapies, Not Elsewhere Classified. Student Contribution Band HECS Band 2 10cp Check your fees via the Fees page. Level Undergraduate Level 2 subject Pre-requisite(s) REHA 1007 Equivalent Subjects REHA 3036 Workplace Learning 2 Restrictions Students must be enrolled in B Health Science in the Recreational Therapy testamur major. This is a specialty subject offered as a compulsory core subject of Bachelor of Health Science-Therapeutic Recreation program. It is profession-specific, preparing students to practice as a recreation therapist and not relevant or appropriate as an elective for non-recreational therapy students. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Conduct an assessment and record findings in a format appropriate for entry into health records
Evaluate and judiciously select evidence-based recreational therapy assessment instruments to determine physical, cognitive, emotional, and social functioning of clients
Conduct and interpret individual assessments based on appropriate models of practice and in line with appropriate ethical standards as espoused by the profession
Collect and analyse data to monitor individual and program progress of goals
Utilise individualized progress notes to record client regression/stabilization or progression toward treatment goals and discharge/referral summaries to record a summary of services and evaluate the effectiveness of treatment received Subject Content Conduct the assessment process 
Apply assessment data to care plan 
Document client progress 
Document program and client incidents
