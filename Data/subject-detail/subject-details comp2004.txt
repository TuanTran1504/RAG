Academic Handbook Current Edition COMP 2004 Computer Networking Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 300565 Coordinator Nabil Giweli Opens in new window Description Computer Networking is an introductory subject in computer systems networking. It covers basic networking technologies, Ethernet fundamentals, ISO OSI model, routing, switching and subnetting, the Internet architecture, networking protocols including TCP/IP, important OSI layer 2 and 3 networking device fundamentals, basic network management and security issues. This subject is also the first of three units, which will prepare students for industry based networking certification (CCNA). They should also have a working knowledge of the World Wide Web. Learning Outcomes Examine the fundamental concepts and protocols for computer networking and internetworking.
Investigate and analyse the Internet architecture.
Analyse the significance of the OSI and TCP/IP models, protocol stacks and their operation in computer networks.
Evaluate modern technologies in computer networking.
Investigate the functions of important OSI layer 2 and OSI layer 3 networking devices.
Assess the management practices and security requirements of computer networks.
Construct, test and troubleshoot a small LAN.
Subnet a small business network given design criteria. Subject Content Assessment The following table summarises the standard assessment tasks for this subject. Please note this is a guide only.
