Academic Handbook Current Edition BLDG 4003 Construction Economics Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200504 Coordinator Xiaohua Jin Opens in new window Description This subject is designed to provide students with: an understanding of economic principles, national and international economic issues; general investment issues; how the national and international economy functions; how the building industry and the building firm relates to the national and international economy; and how economic reasoning may be applied to various problems in the building industry. School Eng, Design & Built Env Discipline Building Construction Economics Student Contribution Band HECS Band 2 10cp Check your fees via the Fees page. Level Undergraduate Level 4 subject Co-requisite(s) BLDG 4012 Industry Based Learning for students in 2607 Bachelor of Construction Management Assumed Knowledge Building construction including residential, light industrial and small commercial as covered in the subjects Building 1 and Building 2 and building measurement as covered in quantity surveying 1 and Estimating as covered in Estimating 1. Learning Outcomes On successful completion of this subject, students should be able to: Contextualise the role of the Australian construction industry in the local and international economy
Explain the supply and demand of goods and services in the general economy and in the context of the Australian construction industry
Discuss the relationship between prices and output in a general economic system
Summarise the national economic policy including fiscal and monetary policy
Develop a business plan for an Australian construction firm
Examine the effect of globalisation on the local economy
Explain competencies required for successful business development using theories and principles of economics Subject Content Construction economics
