Academic Handbook Current Edition LAWS 3032 Intellectual Property Credit Points 10 Legacy Code 200637 Coordinator Sarah Hook Opens in new window Description This subject provides an introduction and overview of the legal principles of intellectual property law, and traces the development of this law in Australia.  It is designed as an introductory survey subject for undergraduate law students. Students will be considering the nature of intellectual property, the legal framework which contains it, the ways in which it can be protected and the reasons why it might seem important to our society to uphold intellectual property rights. The modules will cover the four major intellectual property regimes of trademarks, copyright, designs, and patents as well as issues relating to traditional knowledge of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples.
