- Handbook Summary 2021-Autumn 2024
- This program is aimed at preparing students for a future workforce by cultivating their competencies, knowledge and application of key project management concepts and practices in various related professions.
- It is expected that graduates from the program will be proficient in customising a domain specific project management life cycle and developing comprehensive project plans involving cost and quality control, managing risks and stakeholders in addition to developing efficient schedules while being able to act as effective communicators within a project team setting. Students have the option to graduate with core project management skills only (Graduate Certificate), to continue to obtain generic or discipline-specific project management skills (Graduate Diploma), or to continue to progress with advanced research skills with a Masters’ degree.
- Handbook Summary Spring 2024
- This program aims to prepare students for a future workforce in the dynamic and demanding field of project management. Knowledge, skills and competencies are cultivated through engagement with key project management concepts and practices in related professions, allowing them to make valuable contributions to various industries and sectors.
- Graduates will possess the capabilities to customise project management life cycles for specific domains, create comprehensive project plans involving effective control on scope, time, cost and quality, effectively manage resources, procurement, risks, stakeholders, and communications in project settings, and coordinate all elements of a project to ensure project success with predefined goals being met.
- Graduating with a Master of Project Management opens up a wide array of promising career opportunities across various industries. Some of the exciting job prospects that await graduates with a Master of Project Management include: Project Manager, Project Coordinator, Construction Project Manager, Engineering Project Manager, ICT Project Manager, and Clinical Project Manager.
- Early Exits
- Students may exit this program on completion of 60 credit points with a 3787 Graduate Certificate in Project Management or on completion of 120 credit points with a 3786 Graduate Diploma in Project Management

- Admission
- Minimum admission requirements to this course are an undergraduate degree or higher in any discipline.
- This program has three Pathways (A: Research, B: Coursework, C: Work Placement) with two streams per pathway (generic or specialisation) based on previous study.
- Applicants seeking to specialise in the Construction, Engineering, Information Technology or Health specialisations must have successfully completed an undergraduate degree, or higher, in the specialisation area they wish to study, i.e. construction, engineering information technology, or health discipline areas. Studies in related areas will be assessed by the Director of Academic Program.

- All students must also choose one of the following Pathways (40 credit points).
- Master of Project Management - Pathway A - Research (Testamur major stream)
- In addition to the core subjects, students must complete 100 credit points as follows.
- Master of Project Management - Pathway A - Research (Generic stream)
- In addition to the core subjects, students must complete 100 credit points as follows.
- Master of Project Management - Pathway B - Coursework (Testamur major stream)
- In addition to the core subjects, students must complete 100 credit points as follows.
- 30 credit points of Management alternate subjects.
- 30 credit points of Testamur major alternate subjects from their enrolled major.
- 40 additional credit points of Testamur major alternate units from their enrolled major. This is in addition to the abovementioned 30 credit points of Testamur major alternate subjects.
- Master of Project Management - Pathway B - Coursework (Generic stream)
- In addition to the core subjects, students must complete 100 credit points as follows.
- 30 credit points of Management alternate subjects.
- 30 credit points of Generic alternate subjects
- 40 additional credit points of any alternate subjects (Management, Generic or Testamur major). This is in addition to the abovementioned 30 credit points of Management alternate subjects and 30 credit points of Generic alternate subjects.
- Master of Project Management - Pathway C - Work Placement (Testamur major stream)
- In addition to the core subjects, students must complete 100 credit points as follows.
- Master of Project Management - Pathway C - Work Placement (Generic stream)
- In addition to the core subjects, students must complete 100 credit points as follows.
- Testamur Majors and Generic Stream
- All students must also choose either a Testamur major stream or a Generic stream (60 credit points).
- For the Testamur major stream students must complete three 10 credit point Management alternate subjects and three 10 credit point major alternate subjects.
- For the Generic stream, students must complete three 10 credit point Management alternate subjects and three 10 credit point generic alternate subjects.
